TRANSPORTATION

Sanibel has roughly 17-square miles of land and connects to the mainland by a 3-mile-long causeway. The Island is located ~40 mins by car from the closest Airport – Southwest Florida International Airport (RSW). To ensure that you don't miss-out on any of the fun activities you'll see below, we want you to know your transportation options both to get on, off and around the island.

If you are flying-in to RSW, the best mode of transportation is a rental car. This allows you to have easy access to the hotel and to get around the island to visit the attractions at your leisure. RSW rental companies have good deals on small cars and can go for as little as $20 per night – although if traveling at the same time as family/friends you may be able to save more by getting a larger car that you can share. Hertz also has a car pick-up/drop-off location on the island - great if you want to car-pool to the island, but then have access to your own car. If you are renting a car to drive onto the island please note that there is a $6 toll to get onto the causeway.

BY CAR

Directions from the airport can be found here. Please note that there is a $6 toll to cross the causeway onto the island.

    Route:
  • Take I-75 through Florida until you reach exit 131 (Daniels Pkwy)
  • Use the left 2 lanes to turn left onto 6 Mile Cypress Pkwy (2.9 mi)
  • Continue straight onto Gladiolus Dr (1.2 mi)
  • Use the left lane to merge onto County Rd 869 S/Summerlin Rd via the ramp to Ft Myers Beach/Sanibel/Captiva (3.9 mi)
  • Keep left to continue straight on Summerlin Rd (2.5 mi)
  • Continue straight onto McGregor Blvd (1.7 mi)
  • Continue straight onto Sanibel Causeway (toll road - $6) (3.3 mi)
  • Continue straight onto Causeway Blvd (0.4 mi)
  • Continue straight onto Lindgren Blvd (0.6 mi)
  • The Sanibel Inn is directly in front of you at the intersection of Lindgren Blvd and E Guld Dr. (154 ft)

THINGS TO DO

We know that everyone will be traveling for our wedding so we want you to make a vacation for yourself while you're here! Here are some of our favorite things to do here on Sanibel.

GOLFING AT THE DUNES

The ceremony will be held at the Dunes Resort, which is also home to one of the Island's best golf courses.
DING DARLING REFUGE

Ding Darling's 5,200 acre refuge is the largest refuge on the island. Bike or drive the refuge trails!
TARPON BAY KAYAKING

Tarpon Bay is home to a wonderful kayaking trail amongst the mangroves. Watch for treecrabs!
SANIBEL BEACHES

Sanibel Island has incredible natural beaches, with some of the best shell hunting in the world!
PERIWINKLE PLACE

Some of the best shopping on the Island, lots of different boutiques and island shops.
FISHING

There are a great variety of fishing options on Sanibel, be it from the beach, the pier or on a boat.

RESTAURANTS

There are no shortage of restaurants on the Island, to help you narrow down your options here are a few of our favorites!

THE MUCKY DUCK

Located on Captiva Island a short drive away, is our favorite beachside english style pub!
GRAMMA DOTS

One of the best fried grouper sandwiches on the Island, and Greg's favorite clam chowder.
LAZY FLAMINGO

One of the late night places on the Island with a great beer selection, try the Conch Fritters.
DOC FORDS RUM BAR

Brainchild of the author Randy Wayne White, this restaurant is named for his most famous character.
LIGHTHOUSE CAFE

Excellent option for breakfast or brunch, and a pleasent 30 minute walk from the hotels.
TIMBERS

Great choice for lunch or dinner, and connected to one of the more popular sports bars.
